FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ADHD, stimulants, and focus optimization.

Can I get ADHD medication prescribed online? â–¼
Yes. Several telehealth platforms (Cerebral, Done, Talkiatry) offer online ADHD assessments with licensed psychiatrists who can prescribe medication including stimulants. The process typically involves a video evaluation, questionnaires, and follow-up appointments. Most major insurance plans are accepted.
What's the difference between Adderall and Vyvanse? â–¼
Both are amphetamine-based stimulants, but they work differently. Adderall contains mixed amphetamine salts that act immediately. Vyvanse (lisdexamfetamine) is a prodrug â€” it must be converted to dextroamphetamine in your body, resulting in a smoother onset, longer duration (10-14 hours vs 4-6), and lower abuse potential. Vyvanse is often preferred for its consistency.
Do nootropics actually work? â–¼
Some do, most don't. Caffeine + L-theanine has strong evidence for improving focus and reducing jitteriness. Creatine helps brain energy. Lion's mane shows promise for neurogenesis. However, many expensive nootropic blends contain under-dosed ingredients with minimal research. Stick with well-studied individual compounds.
Is caffeine a stimulant? â–¼
Yes, caffeine is the world's most widely consumed psychoactive stimulant. It works by blocking adenosine receptors (preventing sleepiness) and indirectly increasing dopamine. At moderate doses (100-200mg), it reliably improves alertness, reaction time, and focus. It's legal, cheap, and has decades of safety data.
How do I know if I have ADHD? â–¼
Common signs include: difficulty sustaining attention, frequently losing things, trouble following through on tasks, fidgeting, impulsive decision-making, and difficulty with time management. Only a qualified healthcare professional can diagnose ADHD. Online self-assessments (like the ASRS) can indicate whether a formal evaluation is warranted.
Are stimulants safe? â–¼
When prescribed by a doctor and taken as directed, stimulant medications have a well-established safety profile. Common side effects include decreased appetite, insomnia, and increased heart rate. They are not recommended for people with certain heart conditions. Regular monitoring with your prescriber is important. Never take stimulant medications without a prescription.
